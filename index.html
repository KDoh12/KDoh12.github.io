About

Hello! My name is Kevin Dohner. I served in the U.S. Navy for 4 years and got to go to some amazing places. After that, I attended the University of Kentucky where I graduated with a Bachelors in Geography and a Minor in Mapping and GIS.

While attending the university, I was able to obtain a student worker position with the Facilities Management department at University of Kentucky as a Geospatial Systems Technician in the Geospatial Office. This student position led to a full-time position after I graduated and is where I am currently employed. I am now a Technical Project Specialist I in the Geospatial Office where majority of my time is spent developing GIS solutions and products for various departments across campus, primarily Facilities Management. I am responsible for maintaining various datasets for facilities. One such dataset I am responsible for is the basemaps for the Official UK Campus Map! I have developed some other web maps and apps using the ESRI suite, one of which is the Alumni Paver Locator App.

Redlining in Norfolk

This project compares various economic data from the 1940s to the same economic data from 2019. The redlined zones show how the city was divided up by the Home Owners Loan Corporation (HOLC). By comparing economic data per redlined zone, you can get a sense on how redlining has impacted various parts of the city, and whether those impacts are positive or negative.

Education in Kenya

Over the last couple of decades, Kenya has heavily invested in education. Test scores has risen across the country and there have been several overhauls of their education system. This project showcases the enrollment rate of both boys and girls in Kenyan schools.

Toxic Kentucky

Each year, tons of toxic waste is released into the environment. While many people would like to think these toxins are released far away from their own homes, the truth is that many major cities are home to facilities that release toxins into the environment. This project highlights which facilities and where, in Kentucky.
