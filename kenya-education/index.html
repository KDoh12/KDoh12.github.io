Going to School?

Over the last decade, Kenya has made huge pushes in its education. According to the World Bank, Kenya allocated nearly 20% of its government budget on education. With such a large amount of spending on education, there have been major improvements in enrollment rates as well as test scores in various subjects.

While improvements have been made, there are still various counties in the north and south that are more rural and maintain low enrollment rates. The overall trend of the country seems to show that enrollment tends to decline the closer people get to the end of the education system (grade 8). While I am sure the pandemic did not help in enrollment, the country seems determined to invest heavily in its education.
